# Mortgage Lending Glossary

## Overview

This glossary provides standardized definitions for key terms used across all mortgage lending processes, including loan origination, income verification, and property appraisal. All personnel involved in mortgage operations should be familiar with these terms. Definitions align with industry standards from MISMO, FNMA, FHLMC, and applicable federal regulations.

## Terms

| Term | Definition | Context |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **1003** | Uniform Residential Loan Application (URLA) form number. The standard mortgage application form used by borrowers to apply for a residential mortgage loan. Revised in 2020 to the redesigned URLA. | Loan Origination |
| **Adjustable-Rate Mortgage (ARM)** | A mortgage loan with an interest rate that adjusts periodically based on a specified index plus a margin. Common structures include 5/1, 7/1, and 10/1 ARMs where the first number indicates the fixed-rate period in years and the second indicates the adjustment frequency. | Loan Origination |
| **Adverse Action Notice** | A written notification required under ECOA/Regulation B when a lender denies a credit application, changes terms unfavorably, or revokes existing credit. Must be provided within 30 days of the credit decision and include specific reasons for the action. | Underwriting, Compliance |
| **Amortization** | The process of gradually paying off a loan through scheduled periodic payments of principal and interest. A fully amortizing loan will be paid in full at the end of the loan term. Negative amortization occurs when payments do not cover accrued interest. | Loan Origination, Servicing |
| **Annual Percentage Rate (APR)** | The annualized cost of credit expressed as a percentage, including interest, discount points, mortgage insurance, and certain fees. Required to be disclosed under TILA/Regulation Z. APR allows borrowers to compare loan costs across lenders on a standardized basis. | Loan Origination, Disclosures |
| **Appraisal** | An independent, impartial estimate of a property's market value performed by a licensed or certified appraiser. Required by lenders to ensure the property provides adequate collateral for the loan. Must comply with USPAP standards. | Property Appraisal |
| **Appraised Value** | The estimated market value of a property as determined by a licensed appraiser in a formal appraisal report. Used to calculate the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio. If the appraised value is less than the purchase price, the borrower may need to increase the down payment. | Property Appraisal |
| **Automated Underwriting System (AUS)** | Software that evaluates mortgage loan applications against predefined guidelines and produces an underwriting recommendation. The two primary AUS platforms are Fannie Mae's Desktop Underwriter (DU) and Freddie Mac's Loan Product Advisor (LP). | Underwriting |
| **Basis Points (bps)** | A unit of measure equal to 1/100th of one percentage point (0.01%). Used to express changes in interest rates, yields, and fees. For example, 25 basis points equals 0.25%. | Loan Origination, Pricing |
| **Closing** | The final step in a real estate transaction where ownership is transferred, documents are signed, and funds are disbursed. Also referred to as settlement. The closing agent ensures all conditions are met and funds are properly distributed. | Closing |
| **Closing Disclosure (CD)** | A five-page form required under TRID that provides final details about a mortgage loan, including the loan terms, projected monthly payments, closing costs, and cash to close. Must be provided to the borrower at least three business days before closing. | Closing, Compliance |
| **Comparable Sales (Comps)** | Recently sold properties similar to the subject property in location, size, condition, and features, used by appraisers to estimate market value through the Sales Comparison Approach. Typically three to six comps are used, with adjustments for differences. | Property Appraisal |
| **Conventional Loan** | A mortgage loan that is not insured or guaranteed by a government agency (FHA, VA, USDA). Conventional loans conform to the guidelines set by Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac (conforming loans) or exceed those limits (non-conforming or jumbo loans). | Loan Origination |
| **Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)** | A percentage calculated by dividing a borrower's total monthly debt payments by gross monthly income. Front-end DTI includes only housing expenses; back-end DTI includes all recurring debt obligations. Maximum DTI thresholds vary by loan program (typically 43-50%). | Underwriting, Income Verification |
| **Deed** | A legal document that transfers ownership of real property from one party to another. Types include warranty deed (provides guarantees), quitclaim deed (no guarantees), and special warranty deed (limited guarantees). Recorded in the county where the property is located. | Closing |
| **Desktop Underwriter (DU)** | Fannie Mae's automated underwriting system that analyzes borrower credit, capacity, collateral, and capital to produce an underwriting recommendation (Approve/Eligible, Refer with Caution, or Out of Scope). | Underwriting |
| **Earnest Money Deposit (EMD)** | A deposit made by a buyer to demonstrate serious intent to purchase a property. Typically 1-3% of the purchase price, held in escrow and applied to the down payment or closing costs at settlement. | Loan Origination |
| **Escrow** | An arrangement where a neutral third party holds funds, documents, or assets on behalf of two transacting parties. In mortgage lending, escrow accounts are also used to collect and disburse property tax and insurance payments on behalf of the borrower. | Closing, Servicing |
| **FHA Loan** | A mortgage loan insured by the Federal Housing Administration, a division of HUD. FHA loans allow lower down payments (as low as 3.5%), lower credit scores, and higher DTI ratios than conventional loans. Borrowers pay both upfront and annual mortgage insurance premiums (MIP). | Loan Origination |
| **FICO Score** | A credit score model developed by Fair Isaac Corporation, ranging from 300 to 850. Lenders use FICO scores to assess credit risk. Mortgage lending typically uses the middle score of three bureau scores. Minimum scores vary by loan program (e.g., 580 for FHA, 620 for conventional). | Underwriting, Credit |
| **Good Faith Estimate (GFE)** | A standardized form historically used to disclose estimated closing costs to borrowers. Replaced by the Loan Estimate (LE) under TRID rules effective October 2015. The term is still commonly used informally. | Loan Origination (historical) |
| **Loan Estimate (LE)** | A three-page form required under TRID that provides borrowers with estimated loan terms, projected payments, and closing costs within three business days of receiving a mortgage application. Certain fees are subject to tolerance limits for variance from LE to CD. | Loan Origination, Compliance |
| **Loan Officer (LO)** | A licensed mortgage professional who originates loan applications, advises borrowers on available products, and guides them through the application process. Must be registered with the NMLS and comply with the SAFE Act. | Loan Origination |
| **Loan Product Advisor (LP)** | Freddie Mac's automated underwriting system (formerly Loan Prospector). Evaluates mortgage applications and provides an Accept, Caution, or Not Eligible recommendation. | Underwriting |
| **Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTV)** | The ratio of the mortgage loan amount to the appraised value or purchase price of the property (whichever is lower), expressed as a percentage. An LTV of 80% or less typically eliminates the PMI requirement for conventional loans. Combined LTV (CLTV) includes subordinate liens. | Underwriting, Property Appraisal |
| **MISMO** | Mortgage Industry Standards Maintenance Organization. Develops and maintains data standards (XML/SMART Doc) used for electronic mortgage data exchange between industry participants. MISMO standards facilitate interoperability across LOS, AUS, and investor systems. | Systems, Data Exchange |
| **Origination** | The process of creating a new mortgage loan, from initial application through funding. Includes application intake, processing, underwriting, approval, and closing. Origination fees are charges assessed by the lender for processing a new loan. | Loan Origination |
| **Points (Discount Points)** | Prepaid interest paid at closing to reduce the interest rate on a mortgage loan. One point equals 1% of the loan amount. Points are tax-deductible in the year paid for a purchase transaction. Origination points are lender fees, distinct from discount points. | Loan Origination, Pricing |
| **Pre-Approval** | A conditional commitment from a lender to provide a mortgage loan up to a specified amount, based on a preliminary review of the borrower's creditworthiness including credit check, income verification, and asset verification. Stronger than pre-qualification. | Loan Origination |
| **Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)** | Insurance required by lenders when a conventional loan exceeds 80% LTV. PMI protects the lender against loss if the borrower defaults. PMI can be borrower-paid (monthly or single premium) or lender-paid (built into the rate). Automatically terminates at 78% LTV under HPA. | Loan Origination, Underwriting |
| **Rate Lock** | A commitment from a lender to hold a specific interest rate and point combination for a defined period (typically 30-60 days) while the loan is processed. Lock extensions may incur fees. Float-down options allow borrowers to benefit if rates decline during the lock period. | Loan Origination, Pricing |
| **Title** | Legal ownership of real property, including the right to possess, use, and dispose of it. A title search examines public records to confirm clear ownership and identify any liens, encumbrances, or defects. Title insurance protects against undiscovered title defects. | Closing |
| **TRID** | TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure rule, effective October 2015. Combines and replaces four disclosure forms (GFE, HUD-1, initial TIL, and final TIL) with two new forms: the Loan Estimate (LE) and the Closing Disclosure (CD). Establishes timing requirements and fee tolerance limits. | Compliance, Disclosures |
| **Underwriting** | The process of evaluating a borrower's creditworthiness, the adequacy of the collateral, and the overall risk of the mortgage loan. Underwriters verify that the loan meets investor guidelines, regulatory requirements, and institutional risk policies before approving the loan. | Underwriting |
| **Uniform Residential Loan Application (URLA)** | The standard application form (Form 1003) used to apply for a residential mortgage. Redesigned effective March 2021. Collects borrower demographics, employment, income, assets, liabilities, property information, and declarations. Submitted to AUS for automated evaluation. | Loan Origination |
| **USDA Loan** | A mortgage loan guaranteed by the U.S. Department of Agriculture for eligible rural and suburban homebuyers. Offers 100% financing (no down payment), reduced mortgage insurance, and below-market rates. Income limits and geographic restrictions apply. | Loan Origination |
| **VA Loan** | A mortgage loan guaranteed by the Department of Veterans Affairs for eligible service members, veterans, and surviving spouses. Benefits include no down payment, no PMI, competitive rates, and limited closing costs. Requires a Certificate of Eligibility (COE) and VA appraisal. | Loan Origination |
