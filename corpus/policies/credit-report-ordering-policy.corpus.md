# Credit Report Ordering Policy

## 1. Policy Statement

All residential mortgage credit reports must be ordered through the company's approved credit report vendor(s), must have a documented permissible purpose under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), and must comply with cost control and reissue guidelines established herein. This policy ensures consistent credit reporting practices, regulatory compliance, and responsible use of borrower credit information.

## 2. Approved Vendors and Bureau Selection

2.1. **Approved Vendor:** All tri-merge residential mortgage credit reports (RMCR) must be ordered through the company's designated credit report vendor. The current approved vendor is [Credit Vendor Name], under contract reference VENDOR-CR-2025.

2.2. **Bureau Selection:** The tri-merge report pulls data from all three major credit bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion). The vendor manages bureau connectivity. Individual bureau pulls (single-bureau reports) are not permitted for mortgage underwriting purposes.

2.3. **Score Models:** The vendor must deliver FICO scores using the mortgage-industry-standard score models:
- Experian: FICO Score 2
- Equifax: FICO Score 5 (Beacon 5.0)
- TransUnion: FICO Score 4

2.4. **Vendor Change:** Any change to the approved credit report vendor requires approval from the VP of Operations and the Chief Compliance Officer, with a minimum 90-day transition period.

## 3. When to Order a Credit Report

3.1. **Initial Application:** A tri-merge credit report should be ordered only after:
- The borrower has submitted a loan application with a signed credit authorization
- Identity verification (CIP) has been initiated (SSN verification completed or in progress)
- A documented permissible purpose exists under FCRA (see CRP-POL-MTG-002)

3.2. **Pre-Qualification Credit Pulls:** For pre-qualification purposes, a soft inquiry or single-bureau report may be used with borrower consent. However, these reports are not sufficient for underwriting and must be replaced with a full tri-merge report before submission.

3.3. **Prohibited Credit Pulls:**
- Credit reports must not be ordered for marketing purposes
- Credit reports must not be ordered without a signed borrower authorization
- Credit reports must not be ordered after the borrower has withdrawn their application
- Credit reports must not be ordered speculatively (i.e., before a borrower has expressed intent to apply)

## 4. Cost Controls

4.1. **Per-Report Cost Limits:** The cost of a standard tri-merge RMCR is governed by the vendor contract. Current pricing:

| Report Type | Maximum Cost | Notes |
|------------|-------------|-------|
| Tri-merge RMCR (individual) | $75.00 | Standard ordering price |
| Tri-merge RMCR (joint - per borrower) | $75.00 each | Each borrower is a separate report |
| Supplemental / Upgrade report | $35.00 | Update to existing report within 120 days |
| Rapid rescore (per account, per bureau) | $40.00 per tradeline per bureau | Maximum 3 tradelines per rescore event |

4.2. **Cost Recovery:** Credit report fees may be collected from the borrower as part of the application fee or at closing per applicable state law. The fee charged to the borrower must not exceed the actual cost incurred.

4.3. **Duplicate Order Prevention:** The LOS will flag potential duplicate orders (same borrower SSN within 30 days). Processors must review the flag and confirm the order is necessary before proceeding.

4.4. **Reporting:** Monthly credit report usage and cost reports are generated by the vendor and reviewed by the Credit Operations Manager. Branches exceeding the per-loan average cost by more than 20% will be audited.

## 5. Credit Report Validity and Reissue Rules

5.1. **Validity Period:** A tri-merge credit report is valid for **120 calendar days** from the date it was issued by the vendor.

5.2. **Supplemental Update:** Within the 120-day validity window, a supplemental (upgrade) report may be ordered instead of a full new report. The supplemental report refreshes the data from all three bureaus and extends the validity period by an additional 120 days from the supplement date.

5.3. **Reissue Required When:**
- The original report is more than 120 days old and has not been supplemented
- A significant credit event has occurred (new account opened, balance change > $5,000, derogatory item reported)
- The loan program has changed and the new program requires a fresh evaluation
- The underwriter requests a current report due to concerns about credit changes

5.4. **Reissue Not Required When:**
- The report is within 120 days and no significant credit changes are expected
- A supplemental update has been performed and the supplemented report is within 120 days
- The loan is in a streamline refinance program that does not require credit qualifying (e.g., FHA Streamline, VA IRRRL)

5.5. **Maximum Reissues:** No more than three credit reports (original + two reissues/supplements) should be ordered for a single loan file. If a third reissue is needed, obtain Credit Operations Manager approval and document the reason.

## 6. Rapid Rescore Policy

6.1. A rapid rescore may be ordered when:
- The borrower has made a change to their credit profile (paid off an account, reduced a balance, corrected an error) that is expected to improve their credit score
- The improved score would change the loan eligibility, pricing, or program qualification
- The credit report vendor can verify the change with the creditor and deliver an updated score within 3-5 business days

6.2. Rapid rescores require:
- Documentation of the account change (payoff letter, balance transfer confirmation, creditor letter)
- Loan officer or processor justification for the rescore request
- Maximum of 3 tradelines per rescore event, across all three bureaus

6.3. Rapid rescores are not permitted:
- To remove accurate derogatory information
- When the expected score improvement would not change loan eligibility or pricing
- More than twice per loan file

## 7. Credit Report Data Security

7.1. Credit reports contain sensitive consumer information and must be handled in accordance with the company's information security policies:
- Electronic copies must be stored only in the LOS document management system or approved secure storage
- Physical copies must be stored in locked files when not in active use
- Credit reports must not be emailed to borrowers or third parties in unencrypted format
- Access to credit report data in the LOS is restricted to authorized personnel (loan officers, processors, underwriters, QC)
- When a loan file is closed or withdrawn, credit report access is restricted to compliance and audit personnel

7.2. **Breach Notification:** Any suspected unauthorized access to credit report data must be reported to the Information Security Officer within 24 hours.

## 8. Compliance and Audit

8.1. The Compliance Department will conduct quarterly audits of credit report ordering practices, including:
- Verification that permissible purpose documentation exists for each credit pull
- Review of duplicate order frequency
- Cost control compliance
- Reissue justification review

8.2. Audit findings requiring corrective action will be escalated to the branch manager and documented in the compliance tracking system.
