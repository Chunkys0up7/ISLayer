# Appraiser Independence Requirements (Dodd-Frank Section 1472)

## 1. Overview

Dodd-Frank Section 1472 (codified at 15 USC 1639e) establishes appraiser independence protections for consumer credit transactions secured by the consumer's principal dwelling. These requirements prohibit any person with an interest in a real estate transaction from improperly influencing the appraisal process. The statute supplements USPAP's Ethics Rule (see CRP-REG-PA-001) with specific statutory prohibitions and enforcement mechanisms applicable to lenders, AMCs, and their agents.

All staff involved in the appraisal ordering, review, and loan origination processes must understand and comply with these requirements.

## 2. No Coercion or Undue Influence

### 2.1 Prohibited Actions

No covered person (lender, loan officer, real estate agent, borrower, or any person with an interest in the transaction) shall seek to influence an appraiser or otherwise encourage a targeted value through:

| Prohibited Action | Examples |
|---|---|
| Coercion | Threatening to withhold future business, threatening negative performance reviews, conditioning payment on value outcome |
| Extortion | Demanding appraiser change value under threat of non-payment or blacklisting |
| Bribery | Offering additional fees, gifts, or incentives for a specific value conclusion |
| Intimidation | Aggressive communication, personal threats, or creating a hostile environment |
| Compensation tied to outcome | Fee structures where appraiser compensation varies based on the value conclusion |
| Withholding payment | Refusing to pay the appraiser's customary fee because the value was lower than desired |

### 2.2 Subtle Influence (Also Prohibited)

| Subtle Influence | Why It Violates Independence |
|---|---|
| Including the purchase price in the order with language suggesting it is the target value | Implies the appraiser should confirm the price |
| Stating "we need the value to support the loan" | Communicates a desired outcome |
| Providing only comparables that support a higher value | Selectively influences the analysis |
| Asking the appraiser to "reconsider" without providing new data | Implicit pressure to change the value |
| Multiple revision requests focused solely on increasing value | Pattern suggests coercion |
| Rating the appraiser poorly for values below purchase price | Punishment for independent judgment |

### 2.3 Permitted Communications

The following communications with the appraiser are permissible and do not constitute improper influence:

| Permitted Communication | Conditions |
|---|---|
| Providing property access information | Factual; no value language |
| Sharing the purchase contract (purchase transactions) | Standard practice; appraiser considers it as one data point |
| Requesting factual corrections | Errors in GLA, address, lot size, year built, etc. |
| Providing additional comparable sales data | Factual MLS data only; no value commentary or directive |
| Asking the appraiser to consider specific data | Must be factual, objective, and not presented as a value target |
| Requesting clarification of the appraisal methodology | Seeking understanding, not seeking change |

## 3. Compensation Requirements

### 3.1 Customary and Reasonable Fees

Appraisers must be compensated at a rate that is customary and reasonable for the geographic area and property type. Factors in determining customary and reasonable fees include:

| Factor | Consideration |
|---|---|
| Geographic market rates | County or MSA-level fee surveys |
| Property complexity | Unique properties, large acreage, multiple structures |
| Assignment scope | Interior vs. exterior-only, rush timelines |
| Market conditions | Supply of qualified appraisers in the area |
| Prior fee data | Historical fees paid for similar assignments in the area |

### 3.2 Prohibited Fee Practices

| Practice | Status |
|---|---|
| Reducing fee because appraised value was low | Prohibited |
| Offering bonus for quick turnaround contingent on value | Prohibited |
| Charging different fees based on expected value outcome | Prohibited |
| AMC retaining unreasonable share of fee (appraiser receives less than customary) | Prohibited under AMC regulations |
| Appraiser shopping (seeking lowest bidder without regard to competency) | Discouraged; may indicate quality concerns |

## 4. AMC Usage Requirements

### 4.1 When AMC Is Required

Under the Interagency Appraisal and Evaluation Guidelines and GSE requirements, lenders must use an AMC or equivalent independent appraisal management process to maintain separation between loan production and appraisal ordering. Direct ordering by loan officers or loan production staff is prohibited for consumer mortgage transactions.

### 4.2 AMC Compliance Obligations

| Requirement | Description |
|---|---|
| State registration | AMC must be registered in each state where it operates (per AMC Rule, Dodd-Frank Section 1473) |
| Appraiser panel management | AMC must verify appraiser credentials, licensing, and disciplinary status |
| Quality control | AMC must maintain a quality control process to ensure USPAP compliance |
| Fee transparency | AMC must disclose to the lender the portion of the total fee paid to the appraiser |
| Record keeping | AMC must maintain records of appraisal orders, assignments, and quality reviews |
| Independence firewall | AMC must prevent loan production staff from influencing appraiser selection or assignment |

### 4.3 AMC Selection Independence

The following persons are prohibited from selecting, retaining, or influencing the selection of an appraiser:

| Prohibited Selector | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Loan officer | Loan production staff has financial interest in loan closing |
| Real estate agent (buyer or seller) | Transaction party with financial interest in value |
| Borrower | Has interest in value supporting the loan amount |
| Mortgage broker | Commission-based compensation tied to loan closing |
| Any person whose compensation is tied to loan closing | Financial incentive to influence value |

Permitted selectors: appraisal department staff, AMC operations staff, underwriters (with appropriate separation from loan production).

## 5. Prohibited Practices in Detail

### 5.1 Appraiser Selection Based on Willingness to Hit Value

It is a violation of appraiser independence to:

- Select an appraiser based on a history of providing values at or above the purchase price
- Maintain a "preferred" appraiser list based on value-favorable outcomes
- Remove an appraiser from a panel because their appraisals frequently come in below purchase price
- Use appraiser performance metrics that include "value accuracy" (closeness to purchase price or expected value)

### 5.2 Impermissible Ordering Practices

| Practice | Violation Type |
|---|---|
| Ordering from multiple AMCs and using whichever returns the higher value | Cherry-picking; circumvents independence |
| Re-ordering an appraisal solely because the first value was too low | Coercion by substitution (unless justified by documented deficiency) |
| Requesting a "desk review" to override an unfavorable field appraisal | Improper unless the desk review identifies legitimate errors |
| Providing the appraiser with a "target value" or "minimum acceptable value" | Direct value coercion |
| Conditioning the appraiser's future business on past value outcomes | Indirect coercion |

### 5.3 Post-Appraisal Prohibited Actions

| Action | Status |
|---|---|
| Altering the appraisal report (changing values, removing comments) | Criminal violation (FIRREA) |
| Submitting a fraudulent appraisal to an investor | Criminal violation |
| Misrepresenting appraisal findings to the borrower | Consumer protection violation |
| Failing to provide the appraisal to the borrower (ECOA requirement) | Regulatory violation |

## 6. Reporting Channels

### 6.1 Internal Reporting

All staff are required to report suspected appraiser independence violations through the following channels:

| Channel | Contact | Response Time |
|---|---|---|
| Compliance Hotline | Internal extension or email | 24-hour acknowledgment |
| Chief Appraiser | Direct escalation for appraisal-specific concerns | Same business day |
| Ethics & Compliance Department | For broader compliance concerns | 48-hour acknowledgment |
| Anonymous Reporting Line | Third-party operated; for sensitive situations | 72-hour acknowledgment |

### 6.2 External Reporting

Certain violations must also be reported to external authorities:

| Situation | External Reporting Obligation |
|---|---|
| Appraiser USPAP violation | State appraiser regulatory board |
| AMC compliance failure | State AMC regulatory authority |
| Suspected appraisal fraud | FinCEN (SAR filing); FBI (if criminal) |
| Coercion by loan officer | Internal compliance + state licensing authority |
| GSE appraisal concerns | Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac through designated reporting channels |

### 6.3 Whistleblower Protections

Dodd-Frank provides protections for employees who report appraiser coercion or independence violations. No person may be retaliated against for:
- Reporting a suspected independence violation in good faith
- Refusing to participate in an activity that would violate appraiser independence
- Cooperating with a regulatory investigation into appraiser independence

## 7. Compliance Monitoring

| Activity | Frequency | Responsible Party |
|---|---|---|
| Appraisal order audit (independence checks) | Monthly (10% sample) | Compliance Department |
| AMC communication review | Quarterly | Appraisal Operations |
| Loan officer training certification | Annual | Training Department |
| AMC independence controls assessment | Annual | Vendor Management |
| Appraiser coercion hotline report review | Monthly | Chief Appraiser |
| Regulatory examination preparedness review | Semi-annual | Compliance Department |

## 8. Penalties for Violations

| Violation Level | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Unintentional minor violation (e.g., poor wording in revision request) | Coaching, additional training, documented warning |
| Repeated minor violations | Written performance action, mandatory retraining |
| Intentional influence attempt | Termination, regulatory referral |
| Appraisal fraud (alteration, fabrication) | Termination, criminal referral, SAR filing |
| Organizational systematic violation | Regulatory enforcement action, civil money penalties |
