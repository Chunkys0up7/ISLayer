# USPAP Standards Summary

## 1. Overview

The Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) are the generally recognized standards for professional appraisal practice in the United States. USPAP is developed and maintained by the Appraisal Standards Board (ASB) of The Appraisal Foundation, a congressionally authorized organization. Compliance with USPAP is required by FIRREA (Title XI) for all federally related real estate transactions and is a condition of state appraiser licensing and certification.

This summary covers the key USPAP standards and rules relevant to mortgage lending appraisal review. It is intended as a reference for underwriters, appraisal reviewers, and compliance staff. It does not replace the full USPAP text, which should be consulted for authoritative guidance.

## 2. Standards Rule 1: Real Property Appraisal, Development

Standards Rule 1 governs the development of a real property appraisal. The appraiser must:

**SR 1-1: General Requirements**
- Be aware of, understand, and correctly employ recognized methods and techniques for the property type
- Not commit a substantial error of omission or commission that significantly affects an appraisal
- Not render appraisal services in a careless or negligent manner

**SR 1-2: Problem Identification**
- Identify the client and other intended users
- Identify the intended use of the appraisal
- Identify the type and definition of value (market value, as-is, prospective, etc.)
- Identify the effective date of the appraisal
- Identify the characteristics of the property relevant to the type and definition of value (physical, legal, economic)
- Identify any extraordinary assumptions or hypothetical conditions

**SR 1-3: Scope of Work**
- Determine and perform the scope of work necessary to develop credible assignment results
- Scope is acceptable when it leads to assignment results that are credible in the context of the intended use

**SR 1-4: Data Collection and Analysis**
- Collect, verify, and analyze all information applicable to the appraisal
- Analyze relevant physical, legal, and economic factors
- Determine highest and best use when relevant
- Analyze any prior sales of the subject within applicable timeframes (3 years for Fannie Mae)

**SR 1-5: Approaches to Value**
- Analyze and reconcile applicable approaches to value (sales comparison, cost, income)
- When more than one approach is applicable, reconcile the results to arrive at a credible value conclusion

**SR 1-6: Opinion Reconciliation**
- Reconcile the quality and quantity of data available and analyzed within the approaches used
- Reconcile the applicability of the approaches, methods, and techniques used

### Lending Relevance
Underwriters and reviewers should verify that the appraiser followed SR 1 by checking:
- The appraisal problem is correctly identified (property type, value type, effective date)
- Data collection appears thorough (adequate comparables, market data)
- All applicable approaches to value are considered
- The reconciliation narrative demonstrates sound reasoning

## 3. Standards Rule 2: Real Property Appraisal, Reporting

Standards Rule 2 governs how the appraisal is reported. The appraiser must communicate each analysis, opinion, and conclusion in a manner that is not misleading.

**SR 2-1: Report Content Requirements**
Each written report must, at minimum, contain sufficient information to enable the intended users to understand the report properly:
- State the identity of the client and intended users
- State the intended use of the appraisal
- Summarize sufficient information about the property and the analysis to support the conclusions
- State the appraiser's opinion of the defined value
- State the effective date of the appraisal and the date of the report
- State the appraiser's certifications and any extraordinary assumptions or hypothetical conditions

**SR 2-2: Report Format Options**
- Appraisal Report (most common for lending): contains sufficient detail for the intended users to understand the rationale
- Restricted Appraisal Report: abbreviated format, restricted to the client only (generally not acceptable for lending)

**SR 2-3: Supplemental Standards**
- Fannie Mae, FHA, and VA each have supplemental reporting requirements beyond base USPAP
- UAD (Uniform Appraisal Dataset) standardizes data entry for GSE appraisals

### Lending Relevance
The completeness checklist (CRP-RUL-PA-001) operationalizes SR 2 requirements. Reviewers verify that all required report sections are present, substantive, and not misleading. Reports that omit required sections or contain only boilerplate language may indicate an SR 2 violation.

## 4. Ethics Rule

The Ethics Rule is divided into four sections:

**Conduct**
- An appraiser must perform assignments with impartiality, objectivity, and independence
- An appraiser must not perform an assignment with bias
- An appraiser must not advocate the cause of any party or issue

**Management**
- An appraiser must not accept an assignment that includes a condition that the analysis will reach a predetermined result
- Compensation must not be contingent on a predetermined value or direction
- An appraiser must not allow any person or entity to improperly influence the analysis

**Confidentiality**
- An appraiser must not disclose confidential factual data or assignment results to unauthorized parties
- Exceptions: client authorization, regulatory requirements, legal proceedings, peer review

**Record Keeping**
- An appraiser must maintain a workfile for each assignment for at least five years after preparation (or two years after final disposition of any legal proceeding involving the assignment, whichever is later)
- The workfile must contain all data, reasoning, and support for the appraiser's opinions and conclusions

### Lending Relevance
The Ethics Rule is central to appraiser independence requirements. Lending staff must ensure that no communication with the appraiser (directly or through the AMC) violates the Ethics Rule, particularly the Management section regarding predetermined results and improper influence. See CRP-REG-PA-002 for detailed independence requirements.

## 5. Competency Rule

An appraiser must:
- Determine whether they have the knowledge and experience to perform an assignment competently
- If not competent, they must either:
  - Decline the assignment, OR
  - Take steps to gain competency (through education, research, or association with a competent appraiser) AND disclose the lack of initial competency in the report

**Key Competency Areas:**
- Geographic market knowledge
- Property type expertise (residential, commercial, manufactured, agricultural)
- Valuation methodology appropriate to the assignment
- Applicable laws, regulations, and guidelines (USPAP, GSE requirements, FHA/VA)

### Lending Relevance
Reviewers should verify that the appraiser's credentials and disclosed experience are appropriate for the assignment. Red flags include:
- Appraiser located far from the subject property with no disclosed local market knowledge
- Complex property type (manufactured, mixed-use) appraised by a generalist without specialty experience
- Trainee appraiser without adequate supervisory oversight for the assignment complexity

## 6. Scope of Work Rule

The appraiser must:
- Identify the problem to be solved
- Determine and perform the scope of work necessary to develop credible assignment results
- Disclose the scope of work in the report

**Scope of work is acceptable when:**
- The scope leads to credible results
- The scope is consistent with the expectations of parties who are regularly intended users for similar assignments
- The scope is consistent with the actions of the appraiser's peers performing similar assignments

**Scope includes:**
- Extent of research and data collection
- Type and extent of analysis applied
- Type and extent of data and analysis reported

### Lending Relevance
The Scope of Work Rule is relevant when evaluating whether an appraisal used appropriate methodology. Desktop appraisals, drive-by appraisals, and hybrid appraisals have different scope expectations. The reviewer should verify that the scope aligns with the loan program requirements and the complexity of the property and market.

## 7. Appraiser Independence (USPAP Context)

While Dodd-Frank Section 1472 provides the statutory framework for appraiser independence in lending (see CRP-REG-PA-002), USPAP's Ethics Rule provides the professional standard. Key independence principles within USPAP:

| Principle | USPAP Basis | Lending Application |
|---|---|---|
| Impartiality | Ethics Rule - Conduct | Appraiser must not advocate for borrower, lender, or any party |
| No predetermined result | Ethics Rule - Management | No assignment condition tied to achieving a specific value |
| No contingent compensation | Ethics Rule - Management | Fee cannot depend on the value conclusion |
| No improper influence | Ethics Rule - Management | No pressure, coercion, or incentive to reach a particular result |
| Objective analysis | SR 1-1 | Analysis based on data and market evidence, not desired outcomes |

## 8. Enforcement and Penalties

USPAP violations may result in:
- State regulatory board action (license suspension, revocation, fines)
- Removal from AMC panel
- Removal from GSE appraiser eligibility
- Federal regulatory action (for federally related transactions)
- Civil liability

Lenders have an obligation to report suspected USPAP violations through appropriate channels (state regulatory board, AMC compliance department).
