# GAP-001: Missing Detailed Procedure for Self-Employment Income Calculation

## Description

The "Assess Debt-to-Income Ratio" capsule (CAP-LO-DTI-001) references self-employment income handling in its business rules but does not provide a detailed, step-by-step procedure for calculating qualifying income from self-employment sources. The current capsule states only that "2-year tax return history and a year-to-date profit-and-loss statement" are required, without specifying:

1. How to calculate net self-employment income from Schedule C, Schedule K-1, or corporate tax returns (1120/1120S).
2. How to handle declining income trends (year-over-year decline exceeding 20%).
3. Treatment of depreciation, depletion, and amortization add-backs.
4. How to incorporate business expense ratios and their thresholds.
5. When a CPA letter or audited financial statements are required.

## Impact

Without this detail, agents executing the DTI assessment intent may produce inconsistent income calculations for self-employed borrowers, potentially leading to:

- Inaccurate DTI ratios
- QM compliance violations
- Inconsistent underwriting outcomes across similar borrower profiles
- Fair lending risk if self-employed borrowers are disproportionately affected

## Recommended Resolution

1. Expand the "Income Aggregation" step in CAP-LO-DTI-001 with a sub-procedure specific to self-employment income per Fannie Mae Selling Guide B3-3.4.
2. Add references to IRS Forms 1040, Schedule C, Schedule K-1, 1120, and 1120S.
3. Define the income trending analysis requirements.
4. Specify depreciation add-back rules and thresholds.
5. Consider creating a separate sub-capsule for self-employment income calculation if the procedure is complex enough to warrant it.
