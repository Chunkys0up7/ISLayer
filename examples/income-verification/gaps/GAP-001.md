# GAP-001: Missing VOE Fallback Procedure for Gig Economy Workers

**Status**: Open
**Severity**: Medium
**Identified**: 2026-04-09
**Identified By**: Process Ingestion Analysis
**Affects**: Task_ClassifyEmployment (ICT-IV-CLS-001), Task_VerifySelfEmployment (ICT-IV-SEI-001)

## Description

The current income verification process classifies borrowers as either W-2 employees or self-employed based on employment history records. However, gig economy workers (e.g., rideshare drivers, freelancers using platforms like Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Upwork, Fiverr) present a classification challenge:

- They typically receive 1099-NEC or 1099-K forms, not W-2s.
- Their income is highly variable month-to-month.
- Traditional 2-year tax return analysis may not capture current earning capacity.
- Many have been in the gig economy for less than 2 years, making the standard self-employment history requirement difficult to satisfy.

The current process routes 1099 workers to the self-employment path, which applies Schedule C / Form 1084 cash flow analysis. However, this methodology was designed for traditional business owners and may not adequately represent a gig worker's income pattern.

## Impact

- Gig economy workers may be unfairly denied or receive reduced qualifying income calculations.
- Manual underwriter review volume may increase as these applications trigger variance exceptions.
- Fannie Mae Desktop Underwriter (DU) may return "Refer with Caution" findings for applications with income documentation gaps.

## Recommended Resolution

1. Add a **GIG_ECONOMY** classification to the employment type taxonomy in the RuleEngine.
2. Design a supplemental **Verification of Employment (VOE)** procedure that accepts:
   - Platform-provided income summaries (e.g., Uber annual summary)
   - Bank statement analysis showing regular deposits from gig platforms
   - 1099-NEC/1099-K forms with 12-24 months of platform payment history
3. Implement a weighted averaging method that accounts for seasonal variability in gig income.
4. Consider the **HYBRID** classification path where gig income supplements W-2 income from a primary employer.
